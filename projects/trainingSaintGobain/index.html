An application developed for Saint-Gobain Chalon-sur-Saone, France, in collaboration with Mathis Chartier and Antoine Douchy.


This project was developed for the "Défi Chal'enge 2017", a 1.5 weeks hackathon organised by the Institut Image.

The Défi Chal'enge involves companies established in the Grand Chalon, students of the Institut Image and the business and technology incubator Nicéphore cité.

The Safety training application was awarded "Strength of conviction".




Definition of the project

Saint-Gobain is a glass manufacturing company. Workers share the factory space with sharp glass panels and material handling machines. Each worker already undergoes a classic safety training but the company is constantly looking to improve it. A virtual reality application allows instructors to mentally expose future workers to dangerous situation while keeping them physically safe.



Creation of the application

The application was developed for the HTC Vive with Unity3D (3D environment, interactions and controls), 3DSMax (modeling of objects), Substance painter (texture of the 3D objects).

The worker wears the headset and is asked to move to points of interest in the virtual environment using a Vive controller.

The instructor sees where the worker is in the virtual space and can trigger actions (pass through of a forklift in one of the alleys, movement of transport racks loaded with glass panels or not) by the use of a control panel.
