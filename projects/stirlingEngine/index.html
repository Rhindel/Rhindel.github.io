This study was conducted with Lucas Maigrat at Ecole Nationale Supérieure d'Arts et Métiers, Metz, France.


The following project details the study of the dynamic balancing of a Stirling engine via analytical and numerical analysis.

The study is based on the following blueprint.


The modelling and simulation were realized with Catia V5 and its LMS module.

Analytical study






The analytical analysis allows us to quickly show that the engine is not balanced. By setting out the equations of the balance of mechanical actions, we were able to calculate the force on the crankcase.


Numerical study

Each part of the engine was modelled at scale and organized by equivalence class (by type of movement): both rods (planar movement), crankcase (fix), both pistons (translational motion), crank pin (circular motion).




Aluminium and steel materials were applied to the parts to calculate their masses.


Results

These results were obtained for a speed of 60rpm.

Results are similar with a 5% margin due to approximations in the mass position in the analytical analysis.
