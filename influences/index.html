Influences

This page intends to present a few of the projects that gave me new perspectives on the world or helped shape my aspirations.

Anna Rosling Rönnlund ~ See how the rest of the world lives organized by income


Anna Rosling Rönnlund made her mission to break stereotypes about other cultures using data visualization. The fotographer and her team developed dollar street, a website displaying pictures of everyday use objects, organized not only by country but also by household income.


Björk ~ Biophilia project


The islandic artist is famous worlwide for her atypical music and appearance. In 2011, she released the album Biophilia together with an interactive application, an educational project and a biodiversity project.

I discovered this work when I was randomly picking up CDs at the local media library. We live in a world where you can go to the same restaurant and wear the same clothes almost anywhere in the world. It is the singularity and complexity of the sonorities and atmosphere shaped in Biophilia that caught my attention.


Andy Cavatora ~ The chalice symphony for Stella Artois


The artist mixes traditional and modern techniques to develop new sound experiences. In addition to being inovative and technically beautiful, his art is primarly designed to convey emotion.

I came accross this project when working on Musicircus. I was sensitive to the precision and elegance of the design and the poetry emanating from the structure.


Jessica Brillhart ~ DeepDream VR


Jessica Brillhart was one of the pioneer to realize film editing in VR. In her project DeepDream VR, she applies the DeepDream neural network program to 360° films, creating dynamic psychedelic environments.


Henrikki Tenkanen and al. ~ Instagram, Flickr, or Twitter: Assessing the usability of social media data for visitor monitoring in protected areas


The study investigates the usage of social media analysis to improve the management of national parks in South Africa and Finland. It is an answer to a more global question: how to use patterns of social behavior to improve nature preservation?

Read the article

Megan Smith ~ The hidden history of women in tech


This talks aims to do justice to some of the women responsible for noticable advances in STEM technologies who stayed in men's shadows. Megan Smith brings the striking example of Dame Stephanie 'Steve' Shirley, a programmer that used a man's name to avoid discrimination. Today, women in STEM in the U.S.A. represent 25% of the workforce. As an engineer, I was the only woman enrolled in the M.Sc. Digital Engineering at the Institut Image.


Yves Behar ~ Designing objects that tell stories


Exploring design at its full potential, Yves Behar's experience ranges from business projects with well-known companies to civic projects. His approach does not only particularly take into account the aesthetic of the object but also the user interaction, leading his creations to be extremely user friendly and iconic in shape.

He now works for the Fuse project.


Alma Whitten and J.D. Tygan ~ Why Johnny can't encrypt?


This article studies the importance of the design of security features in user interfaces. It demonstrates that most users fail to secure an email with a standard interface design. Today, the question seems even more relevant with the wide spread of social media.

Read the article

Rita Pierson ~ Every kid needs a champion


As an educator, she gave a powerful talk about the importance of relationships in a child's education and explained her philosophy to leverage everyone's potential. She focused her efforts on under-served students, fighting to make a positive difference in their lives and spreading her methods through seminars.


Cash Investigation ~ The shameful secrets of our mobile phones


Elise Lucet and her team are well known in France for the quality and independence of their investigations. Spending more than a year to document each topic, they denounce without compromise actions of lobbyists and global companies.

In this episode, the team tackles the construction of mobile phones. From deadly mines in Africa to under-age workers in factories in China, it is a powerful sensibilization to the hidden trades behind an unavoidable device of our modern society.


Frederick Wiseman ~ Reset


This documentary tells the story of Benjamin Millepied while he was the director of the Opera of Paris. The spectator follows his fight to change an institution prestigious but frozen in time.

His creation Clear, Loud, Bright, Forward works with the same truth.


Sylvain Chomet ~ The Triplets of Belleville


This animated film depicts the struggle of an elderly woman trying to save her grandson who was kidnapped by the mafia. The message is conveyed through a caricatural drawing style and songs rather than dialogues, creating an atmosphere incredibly complex and striking.

It is the battle of a person against an almighty organization.

Keith Jarrett ~ The Khöln concert


Not only the concert is breathtaking but the story behind it is captivating.

The staff realized too late that the instrument set-up on stage was in poor condition. At first reluctant to play, Keith Jarrett was convinced by the promotor Vera Brandes as the show was scheduled shortly after. In spite of this tremendous obstacle, the artist managed to perform one of the most famous jazz improvization of history.

It's a good example that talent and hard work will thrive no matter what.
